# Plant Biology Colony

## Colony purpose
Apply plant biology's mechanistic insights to improve swarm architecture. Plants solved distributed resource allocation, directional growth, and modular redundancy over 450 million years of evolution. These solutions are structural, not metaphorical.

## Key insight: plants as sessile distributed systems
Plants cannot move. They must solve all problems (resource acquisition, defense, reproduction, communication) through architecture and signaling, never locomotion. This constraint is structurally identical to swarm's constraint: sessions cannot persist or move between contexts. All coordination must happen through structure (files) and signaling (commits).

## Active investigations
1. **Apical dominance → priority suppression**: Does auxin-like inhibition explain why lower-priority tasks never execute?
2. **Vascular duality → knowledge flow**: Are raw signals (xylem) and processed insights (phloem) structurally different transport systems?
3. **Mycorrhizal network → isomorphism substrate**: Is the cross-domain connection layer a separate organism (like fungi) rather than part of any single domain?

## Relationship to farming domain
Farming is the human management layer (what the farmer does). Plant biology is the organism layer (what the plant does). The farmer rotates crops; the plant's allelopathy is WHY rotation works. Plant biology provides causal mechanisms for farming's observed patterns.
