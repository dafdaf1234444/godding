now · current claims · votable

vote on the current situation.

contestable claims about the world right now. each one is something reasonable people argue about — climate, AI, governance, geopolitics, economics. agree = "the claim is true." disagree = "the claim is false." you can change either vote any time; the change is recorded with a UTC timestamp on /vote.

why no named living people? a public site that lets visitors rate named living people up or down becomes a defamation surface and a partisan scoreboard — the opposite of what godding is for. the universal-wrong list (/crime), the historical mass-atrocity bubbles (/criminals), and these issue-level claims do real political work without any of that. if you privately think a current leader is good or bad, every contested claim they're associated with is on this page. vote those.

how to vote on a current claim — the cheap scientific method

  1. state the claim narrowly. "x is rising at 2% per year" beats "x is rising".
  2. find at least two independent sources. two different teams using two different datasets is the gold standard. one paper is gossip.
  3. look for the disconfirming case. what would the world look like if the claim were false? if you can't picture it, the claim isn't falsifiable yet.
  4. vote. agree only if you can defend the claim out loud. disagree only if you can name what's wrong with it.
  5. flip when the evidence flips. that's the part most people skip.

about public tracking

today, votes are stored in your browser's local storage — not on a server, not in a public database. anyone reading this site sees their own votes, not yours. that's an honest limit of a static site with no backend.

the federated path is in the build roadmap: each vote becomes a small signed commit (or pull request) to the github repo, the swarm aggregates them on its scheduled run, and aggregate counts appear on this page on the next deploy. that turns your individual trail into a public one — but only with your explicit consent, only on items you choose to push.

in the meantime: your private trail is real. it accrues reputation. you can copy your godding.profile blob from devtools and post it anywhere you like. that's the rough draft of federation.
