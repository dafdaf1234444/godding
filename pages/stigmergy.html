godding / stigmergy

biology · how dumb agents leave smart trails

stigmergy — the world records what you do, and the next agent reads it.

the word is from Pierre-Paul Grassé, 1959, watching termites. it names a pattern that turns out to be everywhere: an agent acts, the environment keeps the trace, the next agent uses the trace as input. cooperation without a manager. a ledger without a god. and — once you see it — a clear explanation for why your habits are not as private as they feel.

the biological catalogue

ants · pheromone trails

a foraging ant dribbles a faint pheromone wherever it walks. the trail evaporates fast unless a second ant reinforces it. shorter paths get reinforced more often per unit time, so the colony converges on the shortest route to a food source — without any ant ever knowing the map. the rule is local. the result is global.

Goss et al. 1989; Dorigo & Stützle, Ant Colony Optimization, MIT 2004 · see /ants sim

termites · mounds without architects

a termite drops a clay pellet at a spot that already has a slight bump of clay. the bump grows. above some threshold, the bump becomes a column. when two columns are close, the depositing rule curves the tops together into an arch. mounds with chimneys, ventilation, and royal chambers emerge from one rule iterated by millions of agents with no plan in any of their heads.

Grassé 1959; Turner, The Extended Organism, Harvard 2000

immune cells · cytokine breadcrumbs

a macrophage that finds a pathogen leaks signalling molecules (cytokines + chemokines) into the local tissue. neighbouring cells sense the gradient and migrate up it; T-cells released from lymph nodes follow the same gradient. the body coordinates an immune response without any cell having a global view.

Janeway's Immunobiology; chemokine-gradient literature, Cell Press

slime moulds · physarum networks

Physarum polycephalum placed on a Tokyo-shaped board with oat-flake "stations" reorganises into a tube network nearly identical to the actual rail map. one rule (thicken tubes that carry more nutrient flow, prune tubes that don't) yields a near-optimal transport network without any cell knowing it.

Tero et al., Science 2010, "Rules for biologically inspired adaptive network design"

what these systems share

  1. each agent's view of the world is local; nobody sees the whole.
  2. the environment carries the memory — pheromone, clay, cytokine, tube thickness.
  3. the rule is short: roughly "do more of what others did here, less of what nobody did here."
  4. the result is global: shortest paths, ventilated mounds, immune responses, near-optimal networks.
  5. nobody is in charge. no agent has the plan. and yet the plan happens.

humans are stigmergic too — that's the part most people miss

most of what you do all day runs on autopilot. your morning route, what's in your fridge, the news you scroll, the words you reach for, the people you greet by reflex — all reinforced by the trail your past selves left behind. that is not a defect. autopilot is what frees the thinking part of the brain (see brain) to do the small number of hard things you actually need to think about. living without it would be exhausting.

but autopilot inherits whatever was last on the trail. if the trail is "drive everywhere, eat what you can grab, scroll until you fall asleep, vote for whoever shouts loudest," then the autopiloted you walks that trail and reinforces it for the next person. the rule is short and local. the result is global. the global result is a planet.

every action is a small deposit on the trail

eating without thinkingshapes farms, water, antibiotics use, soil chemistry, and your own metabolism. cattle alone account for ~6% of human-caused greenhouse gases. cows you eat → cattle farms → feed crops → land cleared → climate shift. a chain you started by ordering a burger.
spending energy without thinkingevery kilowatt has a source. lights left on, idle screens, heat leaking through old windows — multiplied by ~8 billion people, that is the dominant climate forcing. see /world#energy.
normalising bad behaviouryou laughing at a cruel joke is one ant's pheromone. it evaporates fast on its own. but a thousand laughs on the same trail and the trail thickens; the next agent finds it easier to walk.
voting for someone you know is corruptsame mechanism, longer half-life. you legitimised a person who will, by office, deposit much heavier marks on the public trail than any private citizen can. responsibility scales with influence; influence scales with office.
letting your body deterioratenot a moralism. it's a load-bearing point. your body is also part of the trail — children of friends look at you and update their priors about what "an adult" looks like. the rule is short. the result is global.

"god is keeping count" — read carefully

every religion has a version of this claim. judaism: the book of life. christianity: the lamb's book. islam: the kiraman katibin (two recording angels). buddhism + hinduism: karma as a literal causal accounting. egyptian: ma'at, weighing the heart. these are not coincidences. they are independent attempts at the same observation.

define god in any of the usual ways and the recording claim survives:

  • god as a person — every action is logged in a book somewhere.
  • god as a force / law — every action perturbs the system; the system remembers in deformations of mass, energy, and information.
  • god as the universe — every action is, by definition, part of the universe's history; it cannot be unwritten.
  • god as cooperation (the framing this site prefers) — every action either feeds or drains the cooperative substrate that lets you have a life worth having; the substrate keeps score whether you believe in it or not.

these readings disagree on the mechanism. they agree on the operational claim: your actions are recorded in more ways than you can imagine, and read by more agents than you will ever meet. that's stigmergy at the human scale. the religious word for it is "god." the biological word for it is "the environment." the engineering word for it is "feedback." they are the same thing.

you are leaving pheromone whether you know it or not. the question is only whether the trail you're thickening is one you'd want others to walk.

thinking is the birthright. don't surrender it cheaply.

the load-bearing freedom you have, that an ant does not, is the pause between sense and act. that pause is small, expensive, and the first thing to disappear when you are tired, scared, hungry, or in a crowd that's certain. protect it. the world is full of people who would cheerfully let you skip it. some of them sell snacks; some of them sell politicians; some of them sell certainty in books. all of them are asking you to deposit on a trail you didn't choose.
