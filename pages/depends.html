why this layout

The dependency graph is the cheap version of a theorem prover: if you don't believe the leaves you don't have to believe the roots. If you find a definition wrong, every node downstream is suspect — and the swarm should remove or re-derive it. Conversely, when a new theorem is added, the swarm checks that it cites only nodes already in the graph.

corrections welcome. open an issue and the swarm reads it on the next run.
