reach · about · marketing strategy

reach — how godding gets to people.

a public, automatable plan for reaching the people who want this site to exist. organic only. no paid promotion. the swarm handles most of it on its daily run.

objective · plain

  1. reach the maximum number of thoughtful readers, not just clicks. one careful reader who returns is worth fifty drive-bys.
  2. every step automated by the swarm where legal and ethical. nothing posted on a person's behalf without consent.
  3. compounding, not bursts: ten months of small consistent posts beat one viral spike.
  4. honest framing always. no clickbait, no fake controversy, no impersonation.

distribution channels & cadence

each channel has a different audience and a different rhythm. the swarm produces a share-kit (data/share_kit.json) each daily run with copy-paste-ready text for whatever channels you use that day.

weekly · long

Hacker News

"show HN" post for the swarm + godding combo. focus on the static-site + LLM-distiller-as-knowledge-base angle. one careful submission, never resubmit.

weekly · short

X / Twitter

one short post per week from a single page that landed an interesting diff. usually with the link. let the swarm draft it; you press send.

monthly · longform

Personal blog / Substack

monthly essay drawn from the changelog: "what the swarm flagged this month." longform retention beats spike traffic.

on each diff

RSS / Atom feed

auto-published from the changelog. anyone subscribed sees every accepted edit. zero work to maintain. high signal per follower.

situational

Reddit · niche subs

r/philosophy, r/sustainability, r/MachineLearning, r/changemyview. only post a page when it directly addresses the sub's topic. rarely spam.

on demand

LinkedIn

one post per real product milestone. swarm hits a new metric, a new branch lands, a paper cites the approach.

long-form audio

Podcast / interview

opt-in only. when invited, lead with the swarm story, not the philosophy. people hire ideas they can re-explain to a colleague.

passive

SEO + sitemap

each page is one HTML file with a clean title, a meta description, and clean URLs. no JS-only routing. the swarm checks links each run.

the swarm's marketing job

the swarm is set up to run daily at 09:04 local. on each run it now produces an additional artefact, data/share_kit.json, with:

  • a one-line tweet (≤ 220 chars) drawn from the most recent accepted diff or page edit
  • a 3-line LinkedIn intro using the same diff
  • a Hacker News title candidate (only if a major page was added)
  • a one-paragraph blog stub for the monthly digest
  • the top 5 unedited pages by visit count, in case you want to focus distribution on what's already working

nothing is posted automatically. the swarm only writes the file. you read it; you press send. that's the boundary.

kpis · what we count

  • swarm runs — internal trust signal. should keep climbing daily.
  • accepted diffs — how much the site is being tightened. ratio of accepted/proposed is the quality signal.
  • page-visit profile — local-only on each device, but lets you see which pages a returning reader returns to.
  • github stars + forks — long-tail signal. doesn't matter for a week, matters a lot at twelve months.
  • incoming links — only thing that compounds. keep an eye on backlink reports.

what we won't do

  • buy clicks. not now, not later.
  • name living people on the public site (see /now for why).
  • auto-DM, auto-follow, or any growth-hacking that would be embarrassing if quoted back.
  • SEO games that contradict the content. headlines have to be true.
