justice · the network · proven and alleged

justice — who is connected to whom.

A small, conservative network drawn entirely from convictions, indictments, sentencing memos, and court records. Each line is a public document, not an opinion.

defamation policy. living people are named only when a court has ruled. unconvicted people appear as generic role nodes ("a sitting head of state at the time", "a billionaire associate") — the public record links to them, but godding does not. for the broader claim space see /now.

how to read this

  1. nodes are people, courts, or institutions that appear in a public ruling, indictment, or settled civil judgment.
  2. solid red lines are connections established by a court verdict (criminal conviction, civil liability, or settled with admission).
  3. dashed purple lines are connections from sworn indictments or sentencing memos that are not yet adjudicated. they are alleged, not proven.
  4. godding does not add to either list. the data only changes when a court does.

what this is not

this is not a conspiracy map. it is the opposite — a deliberately small graph that only grows when something is filed in court. if a name is missing it's because nothing in the public record yet links them. that absence is a feature.

For the universal cross-cultural list of crimes see /crime. For the historical at-scale figures see /criminals. To vote on contestable current claims see /now.
