justice — who is connected to whom.

A network drawn from convictions, civil verdicts, sentencing memos, indictments, settled lawsuits with disclosed terms, and unsealed flight logs. Each line cites a specific court document. Vote on whether you think the connection is real — your votes are recorded with timestamps on /vote. The layout is force-directed: drag nodes to reorganise.

godding's two-track naming rule. on opinion-vote pages (e.g. /now) godding never names living individuals — that becomes a defamation surface. here, the question is different: "is this connection in the public record real?" — and the answer comes from court documents that already name the people. So living people appear by name only when there is a verdict, a sentencing memo, a settlement with disclosed terms, an active indictment, or an unsealed exhibit. The category of the edge tells you which: proven = court ruling or settled-with-disclosed-terms; indicted = sworn charge not yet adjudicated (presumed innocent); mentioned = appears in an unsealed log or deposition without any charge attached.

    how to read this

    1. solid red lines — connections established by a court verdict (criminal conviction, civil liability, civil settlement with disclosed terms or admission).
    2. dashed gold lines — connections from an active sworn indictment that has not yet reached verdict. presumed innocent until then.
    3. dotted purple lines — factual appearances in unsealed exhibits (flight logs, depositions). a name appearing in a list is not an accusation.
    4. godding does not add to any of these lists. the data only changes when a court does.

    what this is not

    this is not a conspiracy map. it is the opposite — a deliberately small graph that grows only when something is filed in court. if a name is missing, it's because nothing in the public record yet links them. that absence is a feature, not an oversight.
