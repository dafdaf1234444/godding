criminals · the worst of us

godding's bubbles.

every bubble is a deceased historical figure widely documented as having caused mass civilian death. area is proportional to the rough scholarly mid-estimate of deaths. all numbers are mainstream consensus ranges. click a bubble to read more. a smaller version of this chart appears inline on /crime, right under the cross-cultural list.

death-toll ranges are highly contested by historians; the values used for sizing are mid-points of widely-cited ranges. sizing should be read as rough order of magnitude, not as a precision claim.

why this page exists

crime lists the things humans have agreed are wrong, across cultures, for thousands of years. this one shows what happens when the rule is broken at the scale only states can reach. each bubble is the same short list — killing the innocent, theft, betrayal, harming the helpless — committed by someone with an army or a bureaucracy behind them.

none of these names are controversial in the sense that anyone claims these atrocities didn't happen. they are controversial in the sense that the numbers have ranges, sometimes very wide ones. the chart uses mid-points of mainstream ranges. clicking a bubble shows the range itself. the swarm is allowed to nudge wording on this page; it is not allowed to change the numbers.

these are people whose names every culture would have on its own list, if asked privately. the universal consensus on what's wrong, projected onto the historical record.
