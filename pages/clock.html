godding / clock

framing · the world runs on a tick

clock — the world is a game engine. you are a player, not a prop.

a short manifesto. read it once. disagree on every line if you like. the page exists because too many people walk through their lives as if they were watching a cut-scene. they are not. the clock is real, the inputs matter, and the credits are nobody else's to write.

∆t = real inputs = limited agency = small & load-bearing save-state = the world itself

the engine, in one paragraph

every game engine has a frame. each frame, it reads inputs, advances the physics, updates every agent, renders the world. the world we live in is indistinguishable from this — except the frame rate is set by physics, the agents include you, the renderer is the part of your brain that builds an image, and the save-state is the world itself, which doesn't forget. nobody is at the controller for everything. there is no manager process. nothing in physics demanded your existence; nothing in physics is going to ratify what you do with it.

the clock is real. so is your agency.

most of you runs on autopilot — see stigmergy and brain. the part that doesn't is small, expensive, and the only part the universe seems to care about. it's the pause between sense and act. inside that pause, you choose what to do with the next frame. not every frame is yours to spend — some go to breathing, the people you love, the boss you signed up for. but enough are yours that the cumulative choice is your life.

your environment shapes you. your subconscious shapes you. the family you were born into, the language you think in, the algorithms that decided which faces and headlines you saw before you were ten — all of that is upstream of your "free" choices. that is not a reason to give up on choosing. that is the reason it matters which side of the pause you fall on. the inputs are loaded; you still get to press a button.

"you are nothing" — said by people who need you to believe it

you'll be told this often, in nicer language. nobody cares what you think. you can't change anything. you're a drop in the ocean. just keep your head down. these sentences are not neutral observations. they are inputs, deliberately loaded into your environment by people whose business model depends on you not thinking. recognise the rule and refuse to deposit on that trail.

the honest reading, drawn out across this site (see nothing and belief): you are not nothing, you are nothing organised by ~13.8 billion years of physics until it could read this sentence. you are part of everything. if anything in this universe deserves the word "god" in the sense the site uses it — the cooperative substrate that lets a life worth having exist — then you are part of that thing. so act like it.

two kinds of actor at every tick

you · the small load-bearing kind

thinks before acting, even badly, even slowly. notices when a trail thickens in the wrong direction and refuses to add to it. reads sources before re-posting. votes from the private mind, not the public mouth. takes care of a body and a few people and the small stretch of world they actually touch. boring on the outside; load- bearing in the aggregate.

the bad-actor kind · ignorant, greedy, prideful

skipped the pause between sense and act so long ago they no longer believe the pause exists. take what is in front of them. break what is in their way. they will tell you they are realists. they are not — they have only stopped reading inputs that contradict the trail they're already on. left unchecked they will, frame by frame, consume the people who didn't think. that is not a metaphor. that is the dynamic that produced every entry in criminals.

if you don't think and don't act and just consume — there is a non-zero probability per frame that the bad-actor kind consumes you, the people you love, and the world you needed in order to have anything to consume at all. the probability per frame is small. integrated over a life, it isn't.

what the page is asking you to do

  1. protect the pause between sense and act. it's small, it's expensive, and it's the only part of you the universe is selecting for.
  2. read the inputs you didn't choose. notice which ones were chosen for you. notice who chose them.
  3. deposit deliberately. eat, spend, vote, speak as if your action were a pheromone the next person would have to walk on. it is.
  4. say something. you have a right to speak even when people work hard to make you believe you are nothing. you are not. they are wrong.
  5. find the small group of people who actually pause and act. amplify them. ignore the loud ones who don't. amplification, in a stigmergic system, is the whole game.
