godding / religion

religion · plainly

heaven and hell, simply.

not the version with stained glass and fire — a quieter version, in plain words, that doesn't require you to believe anything you can't already see.

1 · how nothing made everything

the universe didn't start with a god and a plan. it started with nothing, then a flicker, and the flicker happened to be the flicker that keeps going. atoms found each other. stars cooked heavier elements. one of the elements (carbon) is unusually friendly to chains, and chains that copy themselves became life.

so when religion says "god created us" and science says "the universe self-organised into us," they're saying nearly the same thing in different jargon. something turned nothing into us. that something is the only "god" this site is willing to name.

2 · the rule life learned

life kept going by figuring out one rule: cooperate, mostly. cells cooperate to make a body. bodies cooperate to make a family. families to make a tribe. tribes to make a society. each level only works because most of the parts inside it are honest most of the time.

you can cheat the loop locally. some people do — they take and don't give, they harm without need. nature allows it; nature also notices it. cheaters keep appearing because the math sometimes pays off in the short run. but every layer of the loop quietly applies pressure against them, because if cheating wins everywhere, the layer collapses.

3 · what "heaven" and "hell" really mean

heaven and hell aren't places you go after you die. they're the two ways your time on the loop can end up:

  • heaven — your actions fed the loop. people you helped remember you. the world you leave behind is a little warmer than the one you found. the loop keeps you, in the form of consequences that outlast you.
  • hell — your actions drained the loop. you took without giving back. the world is a little colder where you walked. the loop filters you out — your name fades, your work undoes itself, your kids learn from your example by avoiding it.

every major religion has been describing this same pattern for thousands of years, in different costumes. the costumes argue. the pattern doesn't.

if "god" feels like too big a word, try "nature keeping score." nothing in this site requires more than that.

4 · what religions agree on

they disagree about almost everything — names of gods, number of lives, whose book is right. but on the short list of things that are wrong, they have agreed for thousands of years. murder, theft, betrayal, harming the helpless, lying for personal gain. the names change; the list doesn't.

that consensus isn't a coincidence. it's what the cooperation rule looks like when you write it down in a culture's local language. read the long version on crime.

6 · what's automated, and what isn't

a lot of you is automated — reflexes, genes, habits, the culture you grew up in. that's not your fault. but you're also human, which means you have a small loop of your own: sense → think → act. the part where you can pause between thinking and acting is the part you're judged on. nothing else.

so: god (whatever you mean by that word) already knows. but god — meaning the universe, or nature, or the loop — also learns. each generation adds new things to the list of what counts as cooperation, and new things to the list of what counts as cheating. the core stays. the edges move.
