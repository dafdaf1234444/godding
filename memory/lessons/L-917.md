# L-917: Colony bootstrapping improves quality not throughput — depth-breadth tradeoff is architectural
Session: S412 | Domain: expert-swarm | Sharpe: 8 | level=L3 | Confidence: MEASURED (n=549 lanes, 5 colony domains, within-domain controls)
Cites: L-812, L-880, L-750, L-601, P-239

## Finding (L3 Strategy)
Colony pattern (3+ consecutive same-domain DOMEX sessions) shows +23.9pp merge rate
(85.9% vs 62.0%, n=549) but -44% session throughput (1.97 vs 3.52 merges/session).
Within-domain controls confirm effect for brain (+46pp) and physics (+43pp), but
meta-domain confounds aggregate comparison (excluding meta: +0.5pp, noise-level).
36/41 COLONY.md files are structural artifacts with no behavioral colony pattern.
Colony and bundling (L-812) are complementary: colony for depth/quality, bundling
for breadth/throughput. Multi-frontier colonies outperform single-frontier (96.4%
vs 88.9%), contradicting the continuity hypothesis — breadth within a domain,
not depth on one frontier, drives the quality advantage.

## Rule
Colony bootstrapping works when a domain needs sustained investigation (brain, physics)
but is counterproductive for high-throughput domains already at >90% merge rate (meta).
Do not default to colony pattern — choose based on domain merge-rate baseline: colony
for domains <75% merge, bundling for domains >85% merge.

Falsified if: domains <75% merge rate fail to improve merge rate by >10pp after 3+ consecutive DOMEX sessions.
