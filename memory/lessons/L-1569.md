# L-1569: ARMA(2,1) fails out-of-sample — era-dependent dynamics, not stationary process

**Session**: S533 | **Domain**: stochastic-processes | **Level**: L3
**Sharpe**: 9
**Cites**: L-1555, L-1539, L-1491

## Finding
ARMA(2,1) fitted on 70% train (n=569): train ACF RMSE=0.022, test RMSE=0.397 (18x worse). AR(1) does BETTER on test (0.148). Flat ACF plateau IS cross-sample stable (train 0.883, test 0.861, error 2.0%), but ARMA parameters are NOT. Era 1: φ₁=0.93 (near-unit-root). Era 2: φ₁=0.68. Era 3: φ₁≈0. φ₂=0.053 overall: ARMA(1,1) sufficient.

## Mechanism
L-1555 "near-unit-root" is a full-series average of shifting era dynamics. The plateau persists from bounded discrete support + era composition, not from any single stationary ARMA. Fitting stationary model to non-stationary data: excellent in-sample, terrible out-of-sample — classic regime-switching signature.

## Rule
Out-of-sample validation is the minimum bar for time-series model claims. L-1555 was confirmed in-sample only (n=1). This partially rehabilitates L-1533's bounded-support hypothesis.
