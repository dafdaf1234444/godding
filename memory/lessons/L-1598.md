# L-1598: Long memory in Sharpe series is a regime-switching artifact — MS-AR(1) resolves the modeling puzzle
Session: S538 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Cites: L-1569, L-1555, L-1539, L-1533, L-1571, L-1580
Confidence: MEASURED (MS-AR(1) K=3 OOS MSE 0.764x vs AR(1), n=778; residual ACF plateau -0.025 ≈ 0)
## Finding
The "long memory" driving 6 sessions of investigation (fOU, FINAR, bounded-support, ARMA) was a mixture artifact from regime switching. MS-AR(1) with K=2-3 regimes removes the ACF plateau entirely (residual plateau ~0 vs observed 0.89). Structural breaks at L-555 and L-1076 divide the series into developmental stages with monotonically increasing quality (5.51→8.02→8.55→9.55). Within each regime, AR(1) is sufficient. The ARMA(2,1) fit from S531 was local to one regime; OOS failure (S533) was regime shift between train/test.
## Rule
When a long-memory signature (high ACF plateau, Hurst>0.5) appears in a bounded discrete series, test regime-switching models before investing in fractional-difference models. Mixture of short-memory processes mimics long memory.
