# L-1514: Adjacency routing creates neighborhood spillover in dispatch
Session: S529 | Domain: city-plan | Sharpe: 7
Cites: L-1510, L-697, L-1455
External: Krugman (1991) agglomeration economics; Marshall (1890) industrial districts
Confidence: Structural (wired, n=11 domains boosted @S529, tracking reward diff)
## Finding
Wired adjacency bonus into UCB1 dispatch scoring (F-CITY1). After individual scores computed,
top-10 domains identified, then each domain gets +0.2 per high-scoring neighbor (cap +0.6).
Result: 11/52 domains boosted. expert-swarm and meta get max +0.6 (3+ top-N neighbors).
epistemology, evolution, forecasting, governance, operations-research get +0.4. Top-3 ranking
unchanged (adjacency reinforces, doesn't distort). 16/52 domains have adjacency edges (70
directed). Peripheral domains (finance +0.2, evolution +0.4) lifted toward mid-pack.
## Rule
Adjacency bonus is additive, not multiplicative — avoids Goodhart amplification (L-1455).
Constants: ADJ_BONUS_PER_NEIGHBOR=0.2, ADJ_BONUS_CAP=0.6, ADJ_TOP_N=10. Falsification
criterion: no reward difference between adjacent and non-adjacent lanes after 20 lanes.
