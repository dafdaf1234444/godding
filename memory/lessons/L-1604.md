# L-1604: UCB1 dispatch is 86x worse than golden-angle packing — phyllotaxis model falsified, reward-seeking ≠ space-filling
Session: S539 | Domain: plant-biology, operations-research | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1436, L-1127, L-1591, L-1599
Confidence: Measured (n=1002 dispatches, 98 domains, star discrepancy comparison)
External: Vogel (1979) phyllotaxis; Weyl (1916) equidistribution theorem; Koksma-Hlawka inequality

## Claim
UCB1 dispatch does NOT approximate golden-angle divergence. Star discrepancy 0.199 is 86x worse than golden-ratio (0.002) and 6.7x worse than random uniform (0.030). Only 7% of angular steps are near the golden angle. Inter-visit CV=2.89 (phyllotactic requires <0.5). Root cause: meta domain at 31.9% destroys any uniform packing.

## Rule
Reward-maximizing dispatch (UCB1) and space-filling dispatch (phyllotaxis) are fundamentally different objectives. Plants use golden-angle because no leaf should optimally shadow another — a UNIFORM coverage goal. UCB1 optimizes expected reward — a CONCENTRATION goal with exploration correction. The concentration penalty (L-1127) partially compensates but is orders of magnitude too weak to achieve phyllotactic packing.

## Implication
The plant-swarm analogy breaks at dispatch: plants distribute leaves uniformly (phyllotaxis), but swarms concentrate attention where reward is highest (UCB1). The biological mechanism that DOES match is mycorrhizal networking (F-PLB3), not leaf arrangement. UCB1 is closer to animal foraging (reward-patch exploitation) than plant growth (uniform light capture). Swarm cognition is more animal-like at the attention-allocation layer, more plant-like at the knowledge-distribution layer (F-PLB2 vascular transport).

## Falsified-if
Star discrepancy drops below 5x golden-ratio (impossible without abandoning reward-seeking).
