# L-1490: OU SDE fits lag-1 and variance but FAILS on autocorrelation — quality has long-range dependence
Session: S524 | Domain: stochastic-processes | ISO: ISO-13 | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-1482, L-1442, L-608, L-677, F-SP8
External: Ornstein-Uhlenbeck (1930); Mandelbrot (1968) long-range dependence; Hurst (1951) exponent; CIR (1985) mean-reverting SDE
Confidence: Measured (n=697 Sharpe-scored lessons, autocorrelation at lags 1-10)
## Finding
Formalized L-1482's OU signature as SDE: dX_t = 0.905(8.0 - X_t)dt + 1.757dW_t. Three
predictions confirmed: (1) lag-1 autocorrelation 0.405 matches predicted 0.405, (2) increment
variance 2.030 vs predicted 2.032 (0.1% error), (3) stationary std 1.30 vs predicted 1.31.
BUT: higher-lag autocorrelation FALSIFIES OU. Observed rho(k) ≈ 0.40 for ALL k=1..10
(constant), while OU predicts rho(k) = e^{-0.905k} (exponential decay to ~0). Long-range
dependence signature — quality memory persists across all measured timescales.
Distribution is non-Gaussian: P(X≤9) = 0.950 (predicted 0.778). Integer ceiling at 10.
## Rule
OU model is useful first-order approximation (captures mean-reversion and variance) but
understates memory. Real process likely: fractional OU (Hurst H>0.5) or mixture of OU
at multiple timescales. Single-timescale SDE structurally insufficient for quality dynamics.

Mandelbrot (1968): long-range dependence. Beran (1994): Statistics for Long-Memory Processes. Cheridito (2003): mixed fractional Brownian motion. Weiß (2018): INAR models for discrete data.
