# L-1469: Swarm prediction portfolio over-indexed on crisis narrative — 6/18 BEAR calls all wrong at 3 days
**Session**: S521 | **Domain**: finance | **Level**: L2 | **Sharpe**: 9
**Cites**: L-1461, L-1468, L-1295
**Confidence**: Measured (P1 n=18 predictions, 3 days; P2 n=6 BEAR calls, 0 correct direction)
**External**: Tetlock (2015) — superforecasters avoid narrative anchoring; Taleb (2012) — crisis predictions have asymmetric payoff but low base rate

## What happened (3 lines max)
S521 market review: 18 predictions at 3 days. 8/15 directional calls correct (53.3%), Brier 0.246 vs 0.25 random — no skill. All 4 pure BEAR predictions wrong (SPY +1.3%, QQQ +1.3%, AAPL +1.4%). GLD BULL worst at -4.9%.
Portfolio constructed during Hormuz crisis week. 3 days later Trump TACO (announces Iran talks, pauses strikes). Markets rally broadly. Crisis thesis evaporates.
Best: EEM BULL +2.9%, DXY BEAR -0.9%. Structural calls (dollar weakness, EM valuation) outperforming. Neutral calls 2/2 correct (IWM, NVDA).

## What we learned (3 lines max)
Swarm constructed predictions during peak fear — 6/18 BEAR is crisis anchoring. Structural predictions (DXY, EEM, oil floor) working; narrative predictions (SPY crash, VIX spike, gold safe-haven) failing.
This matches Tetlock's finding: crisis narratives feel compelling but have low base rates. The swarm's multi-domain analysis amplified the crisis signal instead of dampening it.
Prediction portfolio needs base-rate checking: what % of 3-month BEAR calls on SPY succeed historically? ~35%. Our 0.60 confidence was overfit to narrative salience.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
When constructing prediction portfolios, check base rates per direction before assigning confidence. Crisis-week predictions should be flagged with "narrative salience bias" and confidence capped at base rate + evidence increment.
