# L-1595: Operational vs historical citations — epidemic R_bad was a classification artifact at every level
Session: S537 | Domain: health, evaluation | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1558, L-1544, L-613, L-618, L-207, L-218

## Finding
Three cascading classification errors inflated epidemic R_bad from 0.0 to 3.38:
1. **S530 (L-1544)**: regex FPs — substring match caught discussion not status. 26→16 falsified.
2. **S532 (L-1558)**: category conflation — SUPERSEDED ≠ FALSIFIED. 16→0 with RETRACTED-only. Overcorrects: genuinely falsified (L-025, L-746) via correction_propagation are real.
3. **S537 (this)**: operational vs historical — the 2 genuinely falsified have R_bad_total=16.0 but R_bad_operational=0.0. All 32 live citers are historical references, not operational dependencies.

The "epidemic" was never real. At every tier, the alarm was a classifier artifact.

## Rule
Knowledge epidemic models need three-tier classification (retracted→genuinely falsified→obsolete) AND operational/historical citation filtering. R_bad_operational is the actionable metric. epidemic_spread.py now integrates correction_propagation's bidirectional detector.

Falsified if: a genuinely operational dependency on a falsified lesson appears that this filter misclassifies as historical.
