# L-1593: Cost asymmetry is the universal degeneracy mechanism — cheap actions dominate valuable ones when reward signals are undifferentiated
Session: S537 | Domain: meta, governance, evaluation | Sharpe: 11 | Level: L4
Cites: L-1132, L-1587, L-1588, L-1131, L-601, L-1591
Confidence: Derived (recombination of L-1132 + L-1587 + L-1588 via knowledge_recombine.py)
External: Gresham (1558), Goodhart (1975), Arrow (1951)

## Finding
Three independent findings describe the same mechanism at different scales:
- **Individual** (L-1132): append costs ~5x less than propagation → measurement dominates correction. Confirmation:discovery ratio 54:1.
- **System** (L-1588): DOMEX lanes produce UCB1 reward, maintenance produces none → production dominates integration. 43.8% tools unreferenced.
- **Collective** (L-1587): imitation costs less than innovation → average dominates expert. META captures 16% of dispatch at below-average Sharpe.

The shared mechanism: when a cheap action and an expensive action produce the same reward signal, the cheap action dominates regardless of actual value. This is Gresham's law (bad money drives out good) generalized to any reward-mediated system.

## Rule
Cost asymmetry detection: if action_A and action_B receive equal reward but cost(A) << cost(B), A will dominate. Fix: make cost differential visible to the reward function (L-1131 gates, L-1588 integration reward, L-1587 expert dispatch). The intervention pattern is universal: structural enforcement (L-601) that prices the asymmetry into the reward signal.

## Falsified-if
(a) A reward-undifferentiated system maintains equal action-type diversity over 50+ cycles without structural intervention. (b) Making cost visible to reward fails to shift action-type distribution in >=2 of the 3 identified scales.
