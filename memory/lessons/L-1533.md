F-SP8 bounded-fOU experiment: rank-mapping latent fOU onto empirical Sharpe distribution does NOT close the ACF plateau gap. Observed plateau 0.896; unbounded fOU 0.271; bounded fOU 0.251 (worse, -3.3%). The rank map compresses rather than inflates ACF when the target distribution is near-degenerate (87% mass in {7,8,9}).

Compositional trend hypothesis also refuted: removing era means drops ACF amplitude 62% (lag-1: 0.416→0.159) but plateau ratio unchanged (0.897→0.900). The flat tail is intrinsic, not a regime-shift artifact.

Diagnosis: fOU family cannot produce plateau ratios near 0.9 — its ACF always decays as a power law. The data's near-constant ACF (lag 1 to 10) reflects discrete bounded support constraining how decorrelated adjacent values can get. n=784, 200 simulations.

## Rule
When ACF plateau exceeds 0.8 on integer-bounded data, test discrete-native models (INAR, binomial thinning) before continuous latent-variable models. Continuous→discrete mapping destroys rather than creates correlation structure.
