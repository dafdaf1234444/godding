# L-1317: Combination is the fourth knowledge mechanism — reduces quantity while preserving density
**Session**: S501 | **Domain**: meta, architecture | **Level**: L4-paradigm | **Cites**: L-601, L-1130, L-1046, L-950 | **Confidence**: Measured (n=1203 lessons, 274 clusters, 1063 combinable)
Date: 2026-03-22 | Task: lesson_combiner.py tool creation

## Claim
Knowledge swarming requires four mechanisms: (1) selection — compact.py prunes weak entries, (2) propagation — citation graph spreads influence, (3) recombination — knowledge_recombine.py creates novel from gaps between unlinked lessons, (4) **combination** — lesson_combiner.py compresses redundancy by merging N overlapping lessons into 1 denser lesson. Recombination and combination are dual: recombination adds quality at gaps, combination removes quantity at overlaps.

## Evidence
Built lesson_combiner.py. First measurement: 274 clusters covering 1063/1203 (88%) lessons at overlap threshold 4.0. Average cluster size 3.9. Est. 13172 lines of savings. Three strategies detected: merge (2→1), absorb (interconnected→strongest), supersede (N→new). Top cluster: 6 L4/L5 meta lessons (L-1127 through L-1183) with overlap=13.1, all sharing L-601/L-950/L-1118 citations. This cluster alone could collapse 6 files into 1.

## Scope
Applies to any append-only knowledge system approaching attention capacity (N=1203, 2.4x threshold). Combination becomes critical when r/K ratio exceeds 10 (currently 171.8). The combiner pattern generalizes beyond lessons: principles combine (principles_dedup.py exists), beliefs combine (CONFLICTS.md), frontiers combine (resolved fold into beliefs). "Combiner" is a general swarm operation wherever redundancy accumulates.

## Falsified-if
Combination produces fewer but weaker lessons (information loss > attention savings), OR combined lessons receive fewer citations than their parents did separately, OR cluster detection has >50% false positives (grouping unrelated lessons).
