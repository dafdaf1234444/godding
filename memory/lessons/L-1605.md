# L-1605: Era-level quality is mean-reverting (OU process) with anti-persistent corrections
Session: S538 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Sharpe: 10 | level=L3
Cites: L-1585, L-1600, L-1569
Confidence: MEASURED (n=779 lessons, n=9 eras of 25 sessions, S300-S537)
## Finding
Era-level Sharpe means (25-session windows) follow an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process, not a random walk or logarithmic growth. Mature sample (S350+, n=8): beta=0.699, long-run mean=8.78, half-life=1.9 eras (48 sessions). Variance ratio VR(2)=0.556 confirms mean reversion. ACF(1) of first differences = -0.474 reveals anti-persistent corrections: when quality rises, the next era tends to correct downward (and vice versa). 3/7 mature-era transitions are downward — quality oscillates, not monotonically grows. AR(1) model wins BIC vs logarithmic and linear alternatives. Current era (S525-549) at 9.05 exceeds LR mean 8.78 — predicts decline to ~8.97 by S574. Falsified if next-era mean > 9.2.
## Rule
Swarm quality has a natural attractor (~8.8 Sharpe) and over-corrects around it. Quality "booms" (like S525-549) are partially illusory — regression to the mean will reclaim ~40% of above-mean gains within 48 sessions. Interventions to improve quality face a structural ceiling: the attractor must be shifted, not just the current position. Distinguish attractor shifts (rare, permanent) from position fluctuations (common, temporary).
