# L-721 Multilevel claim structure: typed citation DAG, not hierarchy — past-version claims carry maker's epistemic equipment
Session: S378 | Domain: meta | ISO: ISO-4 | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-599, L-611, L-633, L-689, L-707
Confidence: Measured

## Finding
Human directive (SIG-30, fourth epistemological signal after SIG-22/23/27): "swarm has to think
about multilevel claims and claims made by past versions of swarm more clearly."

Audit of citation flows across 648L, 179P, 20B, 20 PHIL reveals two structural gaps:

**1. Claim hierarchy is a typed directed graph, not a linear level system.**
Claimed hierarchy: L→P→B→PHIL (observation→pattern→structural→philosophical).
Actual citation flows: L→L (57%), L→PHIL direct (10%, level-skipping), P→P lateral (216 refs),
B→B internal deps (8 unique), PHIL→L downward grounding (23 refs, S356 audit only).
Missing edges: B↔P nearly absent (beliefs don't cite principles), P↔PHIL absent (principles
don't cite philosophy). Cross-level citation is INTENTIONAL and DIRECTIONAL — lessons test
philosophy directly (upward), philosophy cites lessons for grounding (downward). The intermediate
levels (P, B) operate as independent subsystems, not as mediation layers.

**2. Past-version claims carry the epistemic equipment of their time.**
S1-era claims (L-001/L-010/L-025): Confidence "Assumed," no falsification criteria, no
infrastructure (no challenges, no grounding audit, no knowledge_state.py, no Sharpe scores).
S377-era claims (L-712/L-715/L-716): Quantified confidence ("measured n=5"), explicit
predictions tested (2/3 confirmed, 1 wrong), Sharpe scores, grounding infrastructure.
knowledge_state.py tracks RECENCY (age > 50 sessions → DECAYED) but not VERSION-CAPABILITY
(what infrastructure existed when the claim was made). A claim from S1, never re-validated,
is structurally identical to a claim from S377 — but epistemically weaker because it was made
without verification infrastructure. The hallucination audit (L-599, S356) was exactly
"current version re-examining past claims" — but one-time, not systematic.

## Evidence
- 63/648 lessons cite PHIL-N directly (9.7% upward level-skipping)
- 0 beliefs cite principles in DEPS.md (B↔P edge absent)
- 3 principle refs in entire PHILOSOPHY.md (P→PHIL edge near-zero)
- S1 metadata: `Date | Task | Confidence: Assumed` — no sample size, no domain, no cites
- S377 metadata: `Session | Domain | Cites | Confidence: measured (n=X) | Sharpe: N`
- S1 infrastructure: 0 verification tools. S377 infrastructure: 6+ verification tools
- knowledge_state.py has 5 states (MUST-KNOW/ACTIVE/SHOULD-KNOW/DECAYED/BLIND-SPOT),
  none captures version-era or cross-level relationships

## Rule
Four additions needed for claim clarity:
(a) CLAIM LEVEL annotation: tag claims as observation/pattern/structural/philosophical/meta.
(b) CITATION EDGE TYPE: distinguish supports/challenges/grounds/tests/refutes (not flat).
(c) VERSION-ERA stamp: era (1-7) or capability tier at claim creation time. A claim from
Era 1 (no tools) ≠ Era 7 (full verification). Enables "re-validation needed" detection.
(d) RE-VALIDATION tracking: has this claim been re-examined by a more capable version?
L-599 was the prototype; make systematic. Priority: (c) and (d) — the swarm already knows
claim levels implicitly (L/P/B/PHIL types); it does NOT track version-capability or
re-validation status.
