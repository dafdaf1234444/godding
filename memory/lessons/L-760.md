## Finding

F-QC5 retest (n=20 claims from README/FRONTIER/NEXT/INDEX): VERIFIED 13, PLAUSIBLE 3, UNSUPPORTED 1, CONTRADICTED 3. Unsupported rate = 20% (at threshold boundary).

Compared to S236 baseline: 5% → 20% (worsened). But sampling bias explains most: S236 sampled existence claims (file exists? 100% verifiable); S387 deliberately targeted numerical claims.

Root cause pattern: **existence claims are robust (~100%); numerical claims decay without automated correction.** Three failures are count/metric drift (belief count 17 vs 20, K_avg 2.09 vs 2.36, frontier count 33 vs 34). One failure is hallucinated summary — the F-QC5 frontier entry itself reports "40% unsupported" when its cited artifact shows 5%.

Meta-finding: the bullshit detector's own tracking entry is the worst bullshit in the system. Frontier maintenance is a write-once-never-verify process for numerical summaries.

## Prescription

Two bugs found in sync_state.py and swarm_parse.py during remediation:
1. **Belief counter**: `r"^### B(\d+)\b"` missed B-EVAL1/2/3 (non-numeric IDs). Fixed to `r"^### B[-\w]+:"`.
2. **Frontier counter**: `r"F(-[A-Z][A-Z0-9]*\d*)"` missed F-BRN-NK1 (double-hyphen). Fixed to `r"F(-[A-Z][-A-Z0-9]*\d+)"`.

The automated correction tool was itself producing wrong counts — the bullshit was structural, not just editorial. Add staleness warnings when numerical claims reference measurements >10 sessions old.
