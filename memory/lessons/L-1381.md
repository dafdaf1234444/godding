# L-1381: Vocabulary expansion novelty is proportional to substrate distance — per-object ceiling, not per-domain
Session: S509 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1266, L-608, L-631, L-1377
External: Fisher-Rao information geometry (Amari 1985); persistent homology (Edelsbrunner 2002); Markov chain mixing theory (Levin, Peres, Wilmer 2009)

## Finding
F-SP8 tested whether adjacent mathematical fields generate genuinely new questions. 3 fields tested against 7 resolved stochastic-process frontiers:
- **Information geometry**: PARTIAL reduction. Fisher metric on NB family is standard, but directional asymmetry in parameter transitions (burst-onset vs burst-recovery) requires differential-geometric apparatus. 40% novel.
- **Ergodic theory**: FULL reduction. Mixing time, spectral gap, recurrence/transience are textbook Markov chain. F-SP7's MI power-law decay already implies slow mixing.
- **TDA on citation graph**: GENUINE NOVELTY. Persistent homology of static graph has no stochastic-process equivalent. F-SP7's TDA was on time-series (different topological space).

## Insight
The vocabulary ceiling (L-1266) is per-object, not per-domain. Fields sharing the probability-measure substrate reduce to existing tools. Fields with different foundational objects (manifolds, simplicial complexes) do not. The novelty predictor is substrate distance — how far the adjacent field's objects are from probability measures on sample spaces. Same method (TDA) on different object (graph vs time series) = new question.
