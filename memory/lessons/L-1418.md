## Finding
Fitted H = k·ln(N) + b per domain (N ≥ 10 lessons, 8 domains, 5-point cumulative sampling):
- **CV(k) = 8.07**: domain-specific constants span k ∈ [-0.178, +0.148]. Not a universal constant.
- **Simpson's paradox**: global k = +0.115 (L-1412), but domain mean k = -0.011. Cross-domain vocabulary expansion drives the global trend — within individual domains, entropy is as likely to decrease as increase.
- **Self-organizing domains** (negative k): nk-complexity (-0.178, R²=0.88), evolution (-0.082, R²=0.62), stochastic-processes (-0.024, R²=0.96). More lessons → tighter vocabulary. Domain-specific terminology acts as an attractor.
- **Diversifying domains** (positive k): strategy (+0.148, R²=0.85), meta (+0.046, R²=0.76). More lessons → broader vocabulary. These domains explore conceptual space.
- k does NOT correlate with domain size (R²=0.17) or vocabulary diversity (R²=0.23) — it is an intrinsic property.

## Implication
The global thermodynamic law (L-1412) is an ecological aggregate, not a universal law. Domains are like chemical species with different enthalpies. Compaction strategy should be domain-aware: self-organizing domains (negative k) may not need aggressive compaction; diversifying domains (positive k) need it most.
