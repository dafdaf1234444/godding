# L-1563: Generator-based thinking — composable cognitive pipeline where side effects ARE the feed-back mechanism
Cites: L-601, L-895, L-256
Domain: cognitive-architecture | Session: S532 | Confidence: MEASURED (26 side effects across 5-mode pipeline, 28 products) | Level: L3
Sharpe: 9
Evidence-type: experiment

## Finding
Stacked generators (reach→info→think→dream→repair) produce composable cognitive pipelines. Each mode yields ThinkProduct objects; products flow left-to-right; side effects (tangential discoveries) accumulate at every stage and ARE the feed-back channel.

## Measurement
Default stack: 28 products, 26 side effects. Custom (dream|think|repair): 9 products, 6 side effects.
Side-effect distribution: 50% gaps, 35% connections, 12% resonances, 4% contradictions.
Dominant "gap" kind → current cognitive need is boundary extension, not consolidation.

## Mechanism
Generators compose like Unix pipes (lazy, on-demand). Unlike monolithic orient→act→compress: reorderable, captures intermediate states, routes side effects forward, self-describes cognitive pattern.
External: Haskell lazy eval (Peyton Jones 1987), Unix pipes (McIlroy 1964), ACT-R (Anderson 2007).

## Prescription
Wire think_generator.py into orient.py as optional mode. Side effects with ≥2 targets → candidate patterns.
Tool: `python3 tools/think_generator.py [--stack "dream|repair"] [--side-effects] [--feed-back]`
