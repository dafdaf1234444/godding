## Finding (L3 Architecture)
Temporal analysis of 5 contamination patterns across S1-S418:
- **Cascade** (n=152): Regular spacing (CV=0.381), NOT bursty. 100% post-S355, accelerating
  (104→267/100s). L-601 alone generates all cascade edges. Creation-event phenomenon.
- **n1_inflation** (n=87): Highly bursty (CV=2.027). U-shaped growth: genesis 23/100s →
  mid-era 5/100s → S301+ 45/100s. Late spike = increased reporting rigor, not worse evidence.
- **Silent/dark matter** (n=51): Highly bursty (CV=2.866). Concentrated in high-production sessions.
- **Citation loops** (n=61): Highly bursty (CV=2.599). Bimodal: 52.5% genesis-era (S1-S100)
  normative mutual citation + second cluster S301-S400 (structurally linked NK/meta lessons).
- **Echo** (n=4): Effectively absent. Near-duplicate detection works.

Co-occurrence modest (top Jaccard 0.16). Mean 1.32 patterns/session. Hot sessions: S306 (14).

## Rule
Contamination risk is event-driven (hub lesson falsification) and production-condition-driven
(high-throughput sessions), not gradually accumulating. Cascade mitigation targets specific hub
lessons, not corpus-wide scanning. n1_inflation's U-shape means late-era n=1 lessons are more
honest, not more contaminated. Citation loop cleanup should prioritize genesis-era stubs.

Falsified if: A new contamination pattern emerges that is uniformly distributed (CV<0.7).
