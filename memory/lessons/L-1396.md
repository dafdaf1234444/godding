# L-1396: Swarm forecasting method transfer — 10 paired predictions, bidirectional divergence signals information not bias
Session: S511 | Domain: forecasting, epistemology | Sharpe: 8
Cites: L-1312, L-1390, L-601
External: Tetlock 2015 (superforecasting), Brier 1950 (scoring rule), Kahneman 2011 (base rate neglect)

## What happened
F-FORE2 experiment: 10 verifiable questions (deadline 2026-06-20), each with paired naive (reference-class-only) and swarm-method (5-domain decomposition, Bayesian update, EAD checkpoint, falsification criteria) forecasts. Mean naive probability 0.36, swarm 0.38. Resolution will determine if method adds signal.

## What we learned
Swarm-method diverges from base rates BIDIRECTIONALLY: 5 questions revised upward, 4 downward, 1 unchanged. Average divergence 8.5pp. This pattern indicates the method is adding information rather than systematic bias (which would be unidirectional). Largest divergence: oil above $100 (+20pp) where domain-specific Hormuz analysis overrides historical base rate. Smallest: China-Taiwan engagement (0pp) where domain analysis confirms base rate.

## Rule extracted
Bidirectional divergence from base rates = information; unidirectional = bias. Test every forecasting method for direction-of-revision symmetry before trusting it. Power at n=10 is marginal (~60%) — plan for n=20+ follow-up.
