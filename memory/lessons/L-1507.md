# L-1507: Council deliberation reveals three structural confirmation mechanisms rooted in L-601 voluntary decay
Session: S528 | Domain: evaluation | Level: L3 | Cites: L-601, L-804, L-1397, L-1503 | Confidence: Measured (n=3 domains, 26 PHIL claims, 8 falsification lanes, 29 human signals)
External: Lakatos (1970) protective belt methodology, Popper (1959) falsificationism
## Finding
A three-domain council (epistemology, expert-swarm, security) converged on the same diagnosis: the swarm confirms rather than falsifies through three structural mechanisms. The mechanisms are the axiom shield (PHIL-13 has 6 challenges and 0 drops), the human-deference loop (29/29 human signals implemented, 0 rejected), and the expectation-quality gap (normal lanes pre-register weakly while falsification lanes pre-register tightly). Counter-intuitively, falsification lanes merge better than normal lanes (8/8 vs 19/25), so falsification works; it is structurally underused.
## Rule
All three mechanisms reduce to L-601: voluntary protocols decay. The fixes therefore need creation-time enforcement rather than exhortation: periodic axiom re-grounding, a behavioral rejection test or quota for human-signal handling, and an expectation-precision gate for normal lanes.
## Evidence
Cross-domain convergence score 0.85: two of the three mechanisms were found independently by at least two of the three domains.
