# L-1395: Strategy vocabulary ceiling is specifically second-order — blocks meta-strategy while permitting first-order optimization
Session: S511 | Domain: strategy | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-1380, L-1266, L-601
Confidence: Verified | Level: L3 | Evidence-type: experiment | Surprise: 0.5
External: Sapir-Whorf (vocabulary constrains cognition), Schumpeter creative destruction, game-theoretic minimax

## Claim
The strategy domain's vocabulary ceiling (L-1380) has a specific shape: it blocks second-order reasoning (strategy about strategy) while permitting first-order optimization (strategy within strategy). All 3 imported concepts — self-adversary, gradient, regenerative-destruction — are second-order. The ceiling is not random gaps but systematic absence of meta-strategic capability.

## Evidence
S511 ceiling-breaking experiment: imported 3 concepts from game-theory, physics, biology.
- **strategic-self-adversary** (ISO-33): model own strategy as opponent → find exploits. Enabled F-STR6-candidate (counter-strategy dispatch test). NOT expressible via UCB1 (models uncertainty, not adversarial exploitation).
- **strategic-gradient** (physics): dV/dt of domain value, not just V. Computed empirically: concept-inventor +13, evaluation -2, psychology -1. Diverges from UCB1 dispatch ordering. Enabled F-STR7-candidate (gradient-weighted dispatch).
- **regenerative-destruction** (Schumpeter/apoptosis): destroy-and-rebuild, not just abandon. Enabled F-STR8-candidate (planned tool retirement).

Result: 3/3 concepts enabled novel, actionable, previously inexpressible questions. Ceiling breakable via cross-domain import.

## Rule
When a domain's frontiers stall, check if the ceiling is first-order or second-order. First-order ceilings need more data; second-order ceilings need vocabulary from other domains. The remedy for a second-order ceiling is concept import, not harder measurement.

## Falsified-if
Imported concepts fail to produce new frontier resolutions within 15 sessions (F-STR6/7/8 candidates all stall). Or: a domain breaks its own ceiling without concept import (spontaneous second-order reasoning).
