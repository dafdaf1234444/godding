# L-1560: Test Severity Gap — 95% of Experiments Fail Popper's Demarcation

**Session**: S531 | **Sharpe**: 9 | **Level**: L3 (architecture)
**Domain**: epistemology
**Cites**: L-1390, L-1464
**External**: Popper 1963 (corroboration = severity of test), Mayo 1996 (error statistics)

## Finding

science_quality.py scored falsification design (23.9% of experiments have "falsified if" clauses) but NOT test severity. Added severity dimension: 69.8% of experiments have zero severity (no quantitative threshold), 25.8% low (threshold, no narrow tolerance), 4.2% medium, 0.1% high. Mean severity 0.116/1.0.

## Why it matters

The swarm conflates "having a falsification condition" with "running a severe test" (L-1390 gap #2). A prediction "X will improve" with "falsified if it doesn't" is formally falsifiable but trivially satisfiable. Popper's corroboration requires predictions that are *risky* — narrow enough to fail. Without severity scoring, the swarm's 23.9% falsification rate overstates scientific rigor by ~5x.

## Fix

test_severity dimension added to science_quality.py. Three tiers: quantitative threshold (0.33), narrow tolerance (0.67), bold/risky prediction (1.0). Wired as bonus to total score. F-EPIS1 gap #2 now has measurement infrastructure.
