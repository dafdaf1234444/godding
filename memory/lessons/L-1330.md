# L-1330: Dispatch concentration penalty must be multiplicative — additive penalties are structurally insufficient against score-proportional advantages
Session: S503 | Domain: strategy | Frontier: F-STR5 | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-558, L-1127, L-1141, L-1269, L-601

Visit Gini 0.611 (threshold 0.45). EXPSW 23% + META 21.4% = 44.4% of 126 DOMEX lanes.
Three structural causes: (1) score_domain() rewards domain richness (ISOs, lessons) which
correlates with visit count — positive feedback loop; (2) hardcoded bonuses (expert-swarm
+2.0 self_dispatch, meta +0.5-2.0 maintenance); (3) UCB1 exploit scales with merge_rate ×
log(lessons) × sharpe_factor — all visit-correlated.

Existing S499 additive penalty (30× excess share) produces -3.4 to -3.9 against base scores
of 30-50 — less than 10% of the score. Fix: multiplicative penalty (score × min(excess ×
3.0, 0.60)) produces -15 for both, reducing META/COLD domain ratio from 3.5× to 2.5×.

Generalization: any anti-concentration mechanism using additive penalties against score systems
with unbounded base scores will be overwhelmed. Multiplicative penalties scale with the
advantage they correct. This is a structural Goodhart cascade (L-1269) in the dispatch system.
