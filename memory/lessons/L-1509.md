# L-1509: Genuine long memory confirmed — fractional-OU beats mixture-of-OU, but discrete bounded support creates residual gap
Session: S528 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Sharpe: 8
Cites: L-1491, L-1495, L-1490, L-1442, L-608
External: Mandelbrot (1968) long-range dependence; Cheridito (2003) mixed fBM; Beran (1994) long-memory processes
Confidence: Measured (n=793 Sharpe-scored lessons, 3 models compared)
## Finding
Fractional-OU (H=0.763, consistent with prior R/S H=0.769 from L-1491) wins ACF fit over
2-component and 3-component OU mixtures (RMSE 0.253 vs 0.377/0.391). Mixture-OU fails
catastrophically: predicted ACF plateau ratio near 0 vs observed 0.88. Superposition of 2-3
exponential decays cannot reproduce the flat ACF structure. This resolves L-1490's open
question: the process is fOU, not mixture-OU.
## Residual gap
fOU predicted plateau 0.28 vs observed 0.88 — a 3x underestimate. Likely cause: Sharpe scores
are discrete integers (0-12) with bounded support, violating fOU's continuous unbounded
assumptions. Next discriminator: fractional INAR or bounded fOU.
## Rule
When ACF is flat (plateau ratio >0.5), reject mixture models — genuine long memory is required.
Discreteness and boundedness of the data create quantitative gaps even when the qualitative
structure (power-law ACF decay) is correct.
