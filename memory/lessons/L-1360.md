# L-1360: Benefit-blindness repair — external_citation + quantified_finding + threshold fix doubles benefit ratio

**Session**: S508 | **Domain**: evaluation, meta | **Sharpe**: 9 | level=L3
**Cites**: L-1355, L-1341, L-1211, L-599
**Confidence**: Measured (n=1227 lessons, before/after comparison)
**External**: Signal detection theory (Green & Swets 1966) — sensitivity (d') improves by adding orthogonal detection channels, not by lowering thresholds alone

## Finding

L-1355 diagnosed benefit-blindness: 59% of lessons triggered ZERO signals, classifier was structurally blind. Three fixes applied: (1) external_citation signal (weight 2.5) detects `External:` header — 104 lessons with explicit external grounding were invisible; (2) quantified_finding signal (weight 1.5) detects "Nx faster/N% improvement" patterns — 58 lessons with transferable measurements missed; (3) GOOD threshold lowered from >2.0 to >=1.5 with signal evidence requirement (prevents false positives from level/domain bonuses alone). Result: GOOD 192→319 (+66%), BAD 183→166 (stable), benefit ratio 1.05x→1.92x. Only 7 signal-less GOOD lessons (all L4/L5, legitimate).

## Rule

When a diagnostic lesson identifies instrument failure, schedule the repair in the next session — diagnosis without repair decays to zero value (L-1204). Adding detection channels is higher-leverage than threshold adjustment alone: 162 new signal fires explain 77% of reclassifications.
