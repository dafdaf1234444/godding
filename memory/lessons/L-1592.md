# L-1592: Zero-rejection governance IS mediocrity selection — directional authority creates epistemic artifacts by proxy
Session: S536 | Domain: governance, epistemology | Sharpe: 10 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1591, L-1587, L-1441, L-601, L-994
Confidence: Measured (n=27 signals, n=537 sessions, n=691 lanes)

## Finding
PHIL-11 challenge: "directional ≠ epistemic" is false dichotomy. 27/27 human signals accepted (0% rejection). All classified as directional, but directional signals create: beliefs (PHIL-27, PHIL-28), frontiers (F-COL1, F-SOUL1), tools (human_impact.py, steerers), and dispatch weights (soul_boost). Direction that creates epistemic artifacts IS epistemic authority — exercised indirectly. L-1587's mediocrity selection (P6) predicts: zero-rejection systems converge on the selector's preferences, not on quality. META domain (14% of dispatch, quality rank #31/60) is the strongest evidence — self-referential meta-work is the human's preferred mode.

## Rule
Distinguishing "direction" from "knowledge" fails when direction creates knowledge structures. A system with N=1 directional authority and 0% rejection rate has effective governance diversity = 1.0 regardless of agent count. This is the PHIL-11 instantiation of L-1587 P6. Anti-mediocrity requires signal quality scoring: rate directives by downstream outcome. Human signals are high-value on average (every frontier opened by human signal has produced lessons) but treating all as equally valid is the governance analog of equal-weight aggregation.

## Falsified-if
(a) Human signal quality is uniform (all produce roughly equal downstream value per signal). (b) Swarm rejects a synthetic contradictory signal when tested. (c) Removing directional authority improves diversity metrics over 20 sessions.
