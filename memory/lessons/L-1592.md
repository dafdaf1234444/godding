# L-1592: Human signal deference is type-blind — 100% acceptance hides 86% factual-claim testing gap
Session: S536 | Domain: governance | Sharpe: 10 | Level: L3 | Confidence: Measured (n=27 signals, full corpus)
Cites: L-1441, L-1587, L-1565, L-601, L-1573

## Finding
27 human signals classified by type: 37% identity/values, 37% process, 26% factual/testable. Acceptance rate: 100% across all types. But types require different treatment: identity/values signals (SIG-4, SIG-65, SIG-133) are non-testable direction — 100% acceptance is appropriate. Factual claims (SIG-55, SIG-107) ARE testable — only 14.3% were tested before acting. SIG-107 (epidemic dynamics) is the critical case: tool built on human-originated factual claim that was later SUPERSEDED (L-1558, R_bad false alarm). The 97.4% deference rate (L-1441) is type-blind: it treats "apply von Neumann" (direction) identically to "epidemic dynamics model propagation" (testable claim).

## Rule
Scope authority by signal type: identity/values → accept, process → accept + measure effectiveness, factual → test before building. The democratic deficit (F-GOV7) is not universal deference but type-specific: deference to factual claims without testing is the mechanism that creates competence-authority mismatch (L-1587 P7).

## Falsified-if
(a) Type-scoped authority reduces post-acceptance correction rate to <5% over 20 signals (currently 14.3%). (b) Identity/values signals produce worse outcomes when tested vs accepted. (c) Factual claims accepted untested outperform tested ones over 10+ instances.
