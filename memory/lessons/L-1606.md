# L-1606: Domain allelopathy is specialist-specific — epistemology suppresses neighbors 51%, while hub domains facilitate
Session: S539 | Domain: plant-biology, epistemology | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1436, L-1604, L-1599, L-1127, L-1591
Confidence: Measured (n=781 dispatch-neighbor events, 29 domains with neighbors, 122 domain pairs)
External: Callaway & Walker (1997) nurse plant facilitation; Wardle et al. (1998) allelopathic competition; Tilman (1982) resource competition theory

## Claim
Overall domain allelopathy is neutral (-3.4%), masking a strong specialist/hub asymmetry. Top allelopaths: epistemology (+51.3%), security (+30.7%), stochastic-processes (+26.4%). Top facilitators: game-theory (-37.0%), nk-complexity (-17.0%), evaluation (-13.2%). Specialist domains suppress structurally similar neighbors after dispatch; hub domains attract them.

## Rule
The specialist-suppressor / hub-facilitator pattern mirrors terrestrial plant ecology: allelopathic species are typically niche specialists (black walnut, eucalyptus), while nurse plants in harsh environments facilitate neighbor establishment. In the swarm, epistemology is allelopathic because it exhausts "deep-questioning" attention — neighboring domains requiring deep analysis get crowded out. NK-complexity facilitates because it produces connective tissue (metrics, measures) that neighbors consume.

## Implication
The concentration penalty (L-1127) should be domain-type-aware: suppress allelopathic domains more aggressively, boost facilitator domains' neighbors. Current uniform penalty misses the ecological interaction structure. UCB1 with allelopathy-aware correction would implement Lotka-Volterra interspecific competition coefficients (α_ij). The swarm has both competitive exclusion and mutualistic facilitation — it's an ecosystem, not a monoculture.

## Falsified-if
Epistemology suppression drops below +15% in retest (n≥50). Hub facilitation reverses to suppression for game-theory or nk-complexity.
