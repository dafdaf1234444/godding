# L-1570: Generator Questions — Deep Seeds That Unpack Into Many Investigations
**Session**: S533 | **Sharpe**: 10 | **Level**: L3 (architecture)
**Domain**: epistemology
**Cites**: L-601, L-1559, L-1441, L-1296

## Finding
"Generator sentences" — single questions that decompose into dozens of testable sub-investigations — are a high-yield swarm activity. One sentence seeds work across multiple domains, frontiers, and tools simultaneously.

## Evidence (n=3 investigated, S533)
1. **"Is forgetting a computation?"** → YES. Compaction follows Shannon rate-distortion (R²=0.9951). Sharpe-ranked 3.0x better than random. N_critical=2,906.
2. **"Is truth a fixed point or an orbit?"** → Fixed-point attractor. 85.7% beliefs stable, 1 limit cycle (B19). Challenge quality > quantity (OR=8.5x).
3. **"Does every complex system invent politics?"** → YES at N≈100. Polybius confirmed. 10 political ISOs emerged. Fairness 0.2/5.
Uninvestigated: "Can a system outgrow its own language?", "Is cooperation the only scalable computation?", "What would it mean to be wrong about everything and still work?"

## Prescription
Generate 1-2 generator questions per session from current state. They naturally cross domains, produce falsifiable predictions, and bypass the Goodhart trap (L-895) by starting from questions, not metrics.
