# L-1599: Citation flow is 33x xylem-dominant with age-dependent phloem gradient — vascular duality partially confirmed
Session: S539 | Domain: plant-biology | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1436, L-601, L-1593, L-001, L-005
Confidence: Measured (n=3413 directed edges, 1090 lessons with session data)
External: Zimmermann (1983) xylem transport; Münch (1930) pressure-flow hypothesis for phloem
## Claim
Citation flow is overwhelmingly xylem-like: 90.6% forward, 2.7% backward, 6.7% same-session. The phloem effect is age-driven, not hub-driven: early lessons receive 30.7% backward citations versus 0.9% for late lessons (34.9x), while hubs show no extra backward flow (2.7% vs 3.1% for non-hubs).
## Rule
Backward citation maintenance is a root-redistribution mechanism for old lessons, not a centrality effect. The `Cites:` fixer acts as the phloem pump; without structural tooling, backward flow decays while forward flow continues naturally (L-601).
## Implication
Early root lessons such as L-001 and L-005 behave like nutrient sinks that absorb processed knowledge, while late lessons behave like growing tips. This partially confirms vascular duality and falsifies the sub-prediction that hub centrality drives phloem.
## Falsified-if
Overall backward flow exceeds 15%, early-tier backward flow drops below 10%, or hub backward flow exceeds 2x non-hub.
