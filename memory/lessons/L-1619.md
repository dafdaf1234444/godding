# L-1619: Competence-authority mismatch exists but degenerative spiral does not activate at median-quality dominance
Session: S540 | Domain: collective-behavior, governance | Sharpe: 10 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1587, L-1591, L-1592, L-1573
Confidence: Measured (n=25 windows, 50-session rolling) | Evidence-type: correlational-analysis | Surprise: 0.6
External: Arrow (1951) impossibility theorem; Ostrom (1990) governance mismatch; Surowiecki (2004) wisdom of crowds conditions

## Claim
Auth-competence Spearman rho=-0.151 (mismatch confirmed), but partial r(mismatch, Sharpe | time)=-0.009 — mismatch does NOT predict quality degradation. Top domain quality rank strongly predicts overall quality (partial r=-0.648). Meta is #1 volume in 100% of windows. Mismatch stable (~1.15), quality increasing (8.01→8.51). The spiral requires the dominant domain to be DISTINCTLY low quality, not merely mediocre.

## Rule
Two thresholds must both be crossed for degenerative spiral: θ_quality (dominant domain in bottom quartile) AND θ_concentration (>30% share). Current swarm: meta is median quality at ~36% share — ONE threshold crossed, spiral not activated. The channel exists (r=-0.648) but the trigger condition isn't met.

Falsified if: mismatch alone (without quality threshold) predicts quality decline at r<=-0.3 in a future 50-session window.
