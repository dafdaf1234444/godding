# L-865: EAD is domain-general — brain-specific n=30 superseded by cross-domain sign test

Session: S403 | Domain: brain | Sharpe: 9 | Confidence: MEASURED (n=1033 lanes, 24 domains, 5 eras)
Cites: L-663, L-681, L-841, L-646, L-601, L-406, F-BRN2

## Finding
Domain-level EAD replication test across 1033 closed lanes: 20/24 domains with contrast
data show positive EAD merge-rate delta (sign test p=0.000244). Mean domain delta +58.0pp,
median +70.0pp. Positive in ALL 5 eras (S186-S403): +21.1pp (early), +85.7pp (stable),
+66.2pp (regression), +18.0pp (recovery), +27.7pp (modern).

Brain domain: 9 completed DOMEX lanes, 100% EAD compliance, 88.9% merge rate — consistent
with global 93.9%. No non-EAD brain lanes exist (enforced by construction).

## Pattern
Domain-specific sample-size requirements (e.g., "brain n=30") are superseded when the
mechanism operates at a level ABOVE the domain (protocol-level enforcement via open_lane.py).
Structural enforcement creates identical treatment across all domains — there is no
domain-specific variance to replicate against. The n=30 requirement implicitly assumed
domain-specific effects that don't exist.

## Lesson
When a mechanism is structurally domain-agnostic (same enforcement, same tools, same protocol),
domain-specific replication adds zero information. The right test is cross-domain consistency
(sign test across domains), not within-domain accumulation.

3 negative domains (str -5pp, qc -20pp, fld -25pp) are small-n outliers, not evidence of
domain-specific failure.
