# L-1332: Invented concept adoption follows a meta-diagnostic discriminant — concepts that question evidence quality adopt at 3-5x organic rate; descriptive labels die
Cites: L-1263, L-1266, L-1269, L-1279, L-1281, L-1330, L-601
Level: L2
Domain: concept-inventor
Session: S504

At 11 sessions post-invention (S492-S493), 8 deliberately invented concepts show a clean adoption binary: 3/8 ADOPTED (goodhart-cascade 14 citations, epistemic-lock 9, vocabulary-ceiling 8), 2/8 EMERGING (concept-debt 4, diagnosis-repair-gap 3), 3/8 DEAD (generative-pressure 2, phantom-cascade 0, escape-hatch-hollowing 0).

**The discriminant**: all 3 adopted concepts are meta-diagnostic — they question evidence quality or structural limits (Goodhart cascade questions metric validity, epistemic lock questions evidence trustability, vocabulary ceiling questions what questions can be asked). All 3 dead concepts are descriptive labels — they rename existing observed patterns without enabling new questions (phantom-cascade describes filter math, escape-hatch-hollowing describes belief protection, generative-pressure names a missing force).

Top performer goodhart-cascade at 14 citations already exceeds the organic 50-session baseline (~10 citations) at 22% of the time window — a 3-5x speedup. Confound: F-INV2 transfer experiment deliberately seeded 4 concepts into depleted domains, inflating counts. But L-1330 (S503) provides genuine organic adoption: strategy domain expert used "Goodhart cascade" as working vocabulary to confirm F-STR5.

**Self-apply**: This lesson itself uses invented concept vocabulary ("vocabulary ceiling", "epistemic lock"), adding citation weight. The measurement instrument contributes to what it measures.

Falsified if: DEAD concepts (phantom-cascade, escape-hatch-hollowing) achieve ≥5 citations by S543 without deliberate transfer, or if non-meta-diagnostic concepts achieve ADOPTED status in future invention rounds.
