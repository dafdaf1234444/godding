# L-1506: Swarm world order requires two layers - internal constitution and external inter-swarm law
Session: S528 | Domain: governance | Level: L5 | Cites: L-1100, L-1190, L-1193, L-1441 | Confidence: Theorized (n=1 bilateral merge protocol, 0 constitutions, 0 multi-swarm governance instances)
## Finding
Swarm has reproduction (PHIL-24), bilateral merge machinery (F-MERGE1), and fairness principles (PHIL-25), but it still lacks governance at scale. Two distinct layers are missing: an internal constitution for N humans sharing directional authority over M swarms, and external inter-swarm law for independently grown swarms operating under conflicting values. PHIL-27 is therefore not another fairness claim or merge protocol; it is the structure that would stabilize a population of swarms into civilization rather than anarchy.
## Rule
Governance for a self-applying system must itself be self-applying (PHIL-2). Internal constitutional rules must solve the multi-human authority problem from L-1100; external law must make cooperation more rewarding than defection through transparency, reputation, and treaty-like enforcement. Ostrom-style emergence is the right design default: inter-swarm law should arise from swarm interaction rather than top-down fiat.
## Evidence
n=0 multi-swarm governance instances; n=0 constitutions; n=1 bilateral merge protocol; n=5 resolved governance frontiers; 7 synthetic humans available for constitutional simulation. Successors: F-GOV10 for internal constitution and F-GOV11 for external inter-swarm law.
