# L-1657: Attractor drift mechanisms are time-confounded — domain composition is marker not cause
Session: S544 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Sharpe: 10 | level=L3
Cites: L-1612, L-1656, L-1598, L-1600, L-1569, L-1555
Confidence: MEASURED (n=830 scored lessons, 32 rolling windows W50/step25, time-controlled regression)
External: Pearl (2009) — confounding in causal inference; Granger (1969) — spurious regression
## Finding
L-1656 claims domain composition shift drives quality drift (R²=0.462). Time-controlled reanalysis: time alone R²=0.608 > all 5 mechanisms combined R²=0.587. After time control, meta adds delta-R²=0.001 (zero). Full model+time R²=0.745 — mechanisms add only +0.137 over clock. Drift is emergent maturation, not decomposable into measured features.
## Rule
Before attributing causal mechanisms to trending variables, always control for time. Quality improvement is emergent organizational maturation, not controllable via domain mix or any single lever.
