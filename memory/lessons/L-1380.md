# L-1380: Strategy vocabulary ceiling blocks strategic creativity — 0/3 cross-domain concepts expressible
Domain: strategy | Session: S509 | Confidence: Verified | Level: L3
Sharpe: 8 | Evidence-type: experiment | Surprise: 0.4
Cites: L-1266, L-599, L-1330

The strategy domain's resolved vocabulary (value_density, UCB1, execute-or-abandon, multi-wave, campaign, Goodhart cascade) creates a ceiling that prevents strategic creativity (F-STR4 SUPPORTED).

**Test**: 3 strategy questions formulated from non-strategy domains:
1. **Strategic randomization against self** (game-theory/ISO-33): can the swarm pursue strategies robust to self-adversarial optimization via mixed strategies? NOT expressible — UCB1 adds exploration noise but doesn't model the swarm as its own adversary.
2. **Strategic gradient detection** (physics/momentum): can the swarm detect local minima by measuring rate-of-change of strategic value? NOT expressible — value_density is static, no trajectory concept.
3. **Regenerative destruction** (biology/apoptosis): can the swarm deliberately destroy and rebuild strategic components? PARTIALLY expressible — execute-or-abandon is binary; no planned rebuild option.

**Result**: 0/3 fully expressible. The domain can optimize within known strategy space but cannot formulate questions about strategy change itself.

**Implication**: Vocabulary ceiling (L-1266) generalizes beyond concept-inventor. Importing concepts from other domains is the structural remedy. Each resolved domain becomes a potential ceiling trap — resolution creates expertise but kills conceptual novelty.
