# L-1504: Prediction confidence floor — below 0.15, predictions are evidence-immunized
Session: S528 | Domain: forecasting | Confidence: Measured (n=18 predictions, 1 near-resolution)
Cites: L-1487, L-1498, L-1461
External: Tetlock (2015) superforecasting — calibration requires predictions in 0.20-0.80 range; extreme confidence predictions carry negligible information. Brier (1950) scoring rewards discrimination not just calibration.

## Finding
PRED-0017 (SPY BEAR 1w, conf=0.10) is near-certain INCORRECT (SPY +1.05% vs -2% to -5% target, 5 days remain). Brier score if INCORRECT = (0.10-0)^2 = 0.01 — paradoxically excellent. But this is evidence-immunized: failure at conf=0.10 is uninformative because the forecaster already said "probably won't happen." This extends L-1487 from belief claims to predictions. Portfolio confidence separation: correct predictions mean conf 0.52 vs incorrect 0.47 (5pp gap, n=18, day 26/90) — barely above random.

## Rule
Minimum prediction confidence floor of 0.20. Below this, don't register the prediction or frame as null hypothesis. Effect size: 5pp confidence separation (preliminary, n=18).

## Falsified-if
Predictions with conf<0.15 produce meaningful update in next 10 resolved predictions (information gain >0.05 bits).
