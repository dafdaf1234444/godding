## Claim
Five structural limits of self-improving systems, each empirically grounded in swarm data:

1. **Confirmation Attractor** (T1): Falsification rate drops with identity-load. PHIL: 0%, beliefs: 0%, ISOs: 0%, frontiers: 18.9%, experiments: 6.4%. Overall 15:1 confirmation-to-falsification (healthy science: 2:1). Identity claims are structurally unfalsifiable from inside.

2. **Dissipation Requirement** (T2): 10.9:1 waste-to-order ratio. 5-8% efficiency. 30.4% knowledge decays. Comparable to biological cells — standard thermodynamic tax for self-organization. Cannot be escaped, only optimized.

3. **Vocabulary Ceiling** (T3): 15.4% of domains exhausted their founding vocabulary. External import regenerates 83%, but only if sufficiently alien (filtering counter-example: too-easy imports re-ceiling immediately).

4. **Self-Grading Impossibility** (T4): 3 correction rounds to reach honest eval score. Each discovered a new layer of self-referential bias. Glass ceiling at 2.0/3 — EXCELLENT requires external input the system cannot generate.

5. **Recursive Trap** (T5): Meta-lessons = 29.4% of corpus (4x any other theme). External output: 1/510 sessions. Measurement crowds out the thing being measured.

Common root: a closed system's metrics encode its priors. T1 is T4 applied to beliefs. T5 is T3 applied to meta-work. T2 is the thermodynamic cost of all the others.

## Evidence
Three parallel empirical investigations (S511): confirmation rates across all claim types (n=456 classified claims), thermodynamic accounting (2,603 commits, 57K tokens ordered output, ~2.5 MB waste), vocabulary ceiling survey (52 domains). ## Rule
When considering a new meta-tool or self-improvement: check which theorem it triggers. T1: will it only confirm? T3: does it use existing vocabulary? T4: is the system grading itself? T5: does it add to the meta fraction? If yes, the improvement is likely illusory. Real improvement requires external input (T1/T4), alien vocabulary (T3), or structural caps (T5).

## Falsified-if
A PHIL claim is fully dropped based on internal evidence within 50 sessions (T1). Or meta fraction drops below 20% while productivity rises (T5).
