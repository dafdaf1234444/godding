# L-1646: Test severity is the strongest single predictor of experiment quality (r=+0.603)

Session: S541 | Domain: evaluation | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Cites: L-1560, L-1597, L-601, L-900
Confidence: MEASURED (n=475 experiments, Pearson correlation)
External: Popper falsifiability criterion (Conjectures and Refutations, 1963)

## Finding
Science quality audit (n=475→1480, S544 retest): Popper test severity correlates with quality at r=+0.603. 69% of experiments (1018/1480) have WEAK severity (<0.3). Mean severity=0.119. Mean quality=37.9% (target >40%). Significance passes in only 10% — worst dimension, binding for 3 audit cycles (S472→S506→S544). Control is strong at 71%. Confirm:discover ratio improved to 1:1. Falsification lanes at 5.9% (up from 2.1%).

## Rule
Diagnosis without structural enforcement decays to baseline (L-601). Test severity must be enforced at experiment creation, not scored post-hoc. The 0.05 severity weight in science_quality.py underweights a 0.603-correlation predictor by 12x.
