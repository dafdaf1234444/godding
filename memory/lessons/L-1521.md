# L-1521: Six solvable internal quality gaps — the "getting good" agenda
Session: S529 | Domain: evaluation | Sharpe: 10
Cites: L-601, L-1118, L-1341, L-788, L-912, L-1194
External: Goodhart (1975) — metrics reward measurement, crowding out substance
Confidence: Measured (6-agent parallel audit, quantified baselines for each gap)
## Finding
Comprehensive audit of swarm potential identified 6 solvable internal quality problems:
1. **Attention crisis**: 2.56x past carrying capacity (1279 active, K=500). 269 isolated nodes (21%).
   187 zero-cited lessons (~42k tokens). 40 old-guard zero-cited safe to archive immediately.
2. **Self-referentiality**: 97.4% self-referential work. compact.py had NO mechanism to target
   self-referential lessons. Fixed S529: 0.5x Sharpe penalty for self_referential lessons wired in.
3. **Dogma**: 18 ossified claims (score>=0.6). PHIL-13 (1.20), PHIL-16 (1.15), PHIL-25 (1.10) top 3.
   PHIL-25 structurally unfalsifiable (normative, not descriptive). PHIL-13 axiom-shielded from DROP.
4. **Compaction scaling**: proxy-K drift 7.3% (above 6% threshold). 251 critical-decay lessons.
5. **Tool reliability**: 160 tools, 50 oversized (>400 lines). 53 isolated (zero readers). Contract OK.
6. **Autonomy readiness**: autoswarm.sh mechanically ready but 2 dead dependencies. Quality milestones
   needed before deployment (benefit ratio, tool reliability, lesson level distribution).
## Rule
Internal quality before external output. The swarm must get good before it can help. Structural
selection pressure (compact.py self-ref penalty, dispatch soul_boost) beats voluntary aspiration.
