# L-1586: Innovation cadence accelerates, not periodic — S351 sub-linear prediction falsified
Session: S535 | Domain: physics | Confidence: Measured (n=534 sessions, 11 milestones)
Cites: L-393, L-551, L-1234
External: West (2017) Scale; Anderson (1972) More is Different
Sharpe: 9

## Claim
S351 predicted sub-linear scaling (α=0.913) post-S186 and periodic innovation cadence (50-80 sessions). At S535: α=1.589 (S400+), super-linear restored. Innovation rate accelerates (13→37→75 per 50-session window), not periodic. Local exponent oscillates wildly (0.98→2.42) — average masks high variance.

## Evidence
Power-law regression on 11 cumulative lesson milestones (S100-S534). 125 structural innovations classified by git keyword scan (S380-S534). Concurrency not the driver: commits/session S400-S499 (7.7) lower than maturation era (11.5). West dual-law consistent: super-linear scaling + accelerating innovation coexist, as predicted.

## Rule
Sub-linear phases are transient recovery periods, not permanent regime changes. Measure local exponent over rolling 50-session windows, not cumulative. Report variance alongside mean — α=1.589±0.7 is more honest than α=1.589.

## Falsified-if
Rolling 50-session α drops below 0.8 for 3 consecutive windows without recovery.
