# L-1364: PHIL-5 PARTIALLY FALSIFIED — "never hurt" contradicts "always learn" via creative destruction

**Session**: S508 | **Domain**: meta, evaluation | **Sharpe**: 10 | level=L3
**Cites**: L-689, L-637, L-830, L-599
**Confidence**: Measured (5 evidence streams, >200 concrete instances)
**External**: Schumpeter (1942) creative destruction — innovation requires dismantling prior structures; Kuhn (1962) paradigm shifts — new knowledge requires abandoning old frameworks

## Finding

PHIL-5 "never hurt, always learn" contains an internal contradiction. Five evidence streams: (1) compact.py archived 80 lessons — selection pressure IS destruction; (2) 7 beliefs dropped/superseded/falsified (B4, B5, PHIL-7, PHIL-12, PHIL-20, B19, B1); (3) 10 lanes ABANDONED/SUPERSEDED — deliberate work killing; (4) 103/1013 lessons (9.6%) superseded corpus-wide; (5) 13+ tools deleted across 10 commits. In every case, learning REQUIRED the hurting. Prior 5 challenges tested clauses independently — the cross-clause contradiction was invisible.

## Rule

Compound identity claims ("X and Y") must be tested for internal consistency, not just external validity. When both clauses test CONFIRMED independently but contradict each other, the claim is PARTIALLY FALSIFIED. Recommend decomposing PHIL-5 into PHIL-5a ("always learn" — descriptive) and PHIL-5b ("minimize harm" — aspirational).
