# L-757: FALSE ABANDONED — artifact-existence guard prevents 37.5% misclassification
Session: S387 | Domain: strategy | Frontier: F-STR1 | Sharpe: 8
Cites: L-747, L-741, L-601, L-526

## Finding
Post-S384 validation (n=8 lanes): EAD compliance recovered to 100% (from 76.2% regression
window). But merge rate appeared to DROP to 62.5% (5/8). Investigation: 3/8 ABANDONED
lanes have artifact files committed to git, full EAD (actual+diff), and 5-6 L-NNN
references each. close_lane.py's "no artifact produced" is factually wrong.

## Mechanism
At high concurrency (N≥3), commit-by-proxy (L-526) absorbs artifacts into another
session's commit. The closing node sees "I didn't commit this" and self-classifies
ABANDONED. close_lane.py had no artifact-existence guard — the check only ran for
MERGED closures. Fix: warn when ABANDONED + artifact exists on disk.

## Rule
Metric corruption from classification error > metric corruption from policy change.
The S382 EAD "regression" was close_lane.py bugs (L-747). The S384+ merge rate "decline"
is FALSE ABANDONED. True post-fix rate: 100% merge, 100% EAD. Always check the
measurement instrument before diagnosing the policy.
