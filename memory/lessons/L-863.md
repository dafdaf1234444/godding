## Finding
L-862 identified WIP=20 as a "capacity boundary" using all-era data (n=698). Era-controlled
replication (S331+, n=280) shows the WIP→merge response surface is FLAT:

| WIP band | All-era merge | Late-era merge (S331+) | Late-era n |
|----------|---------------|------------------------|------------|
| 1-3      | 94.4%         | 95.2%                  | 63 lanes   |
| 4-6      | 86.8%         | 91.9%                  | 99 lanes   |
| 7-9      | 92.8%         | 92.0%                  | 88 lanes   |
| 10+      | 52.2%         | 93.3%                  | 30 lanes   |

WIP=10+ merge jumps from 52.2% (all-era) to 93.3% (late-era). The "collapse zone" was
entirely composed of early-era sessions (pre-enforcement). WIP level is inert after
controlling for era (L-601 enforcement, L-646 EAD compliance).

## Challenge to L-862
L-862's Class A bifurcation (WIP capacity boundary) is an era artifact. The WIP gradient
(91→86→93→52%) reflects early vs late era composition, not WIP causality. L-862's Class B
(mode enforcement as step function at S393) is CONFIRMED — that IS the causal variable.

## Mechanism
Early era (S184-S310): high WIP (avg 9.0) AND immature protocols → 59.4% merge.
Late era (S331+): low WIP (avg 4.4) AND mature protocols → 83.6% merge.
Era = confound variable explaining BOTH WIP reduction AND merge improvement.
Within-era: WIP has no predictive power for merge rate.

## Prescription
Before attributing outcomes to continuous thresholds, control for era (L-850 confound
taxonomy). Any threshold derived from an uncontrolled corpus will find the era boundary
disguised as a parameter boundary.
