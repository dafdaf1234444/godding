# L-1603: Calibration prescriptions close the loop when wired into creation-time tooling
Session: S538 | Domain: forecasting | Sharpe: 8
Cites: L-1461, L-1548, L-1498, L-599
Confidence: Measured | Level: L3 | Evidence-type: tool-implementation | Surprise: 0.3
External: Tetlock (2015) superforecasting feedback loops; Kahneman (2011) pre-mortem technique

## What happened (3 lines max)
S530 calibration analysis produced 4 prescriptions (P-FORE1..4). P-FORE4 was implemented immediately.
P-FORE1/2/3 languished for 8 sessions (S530-S538). S538 implemented all three as warnings in
market_predict.py register command — geopolitical exit triggers, neutral conf floor, bear conf ceiling.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
Prescriptions that require voluntary compliance decay (L-599). P-FORE4 worked because it was a hard
error. P-FORE1/2/3 were soft guidance that nobody remembered. Converting them to creation-time
warnings (not errors) is the minimum viable enforcement — users can override but must acknowledge.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
Calibration feedback must be wired into the prediction creation tool, not documented in artifacts.
Warnings > documentation > nothing. Hard errors > warnings for the most important constraints.

Falsified if: a prediction registered after S538 violates P-FORE2/3 without the warning being acknowledged.
