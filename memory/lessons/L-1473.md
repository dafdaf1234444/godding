## What happened (3 lines max)
NAT scan at S521 (within S520-S540 window). Predicted 0-2 new FMs based on declining global rate. Found 6 candidates: 2 HIGH (FM-45 import brittleness, FM-46 test-severity inversion), 4 MEDIUM (FM-47-50 across epistemology and scale-monitoring).
Infrastructure layer (FM-01 through FM-14) generated 0 new FMs in 40 sessions. Epistemology layer (FM-21+) generated 4 of 6 candidates.
Previous NAT predictions assumed single global rate. Each failure surface has independent FM generation rate following its own maturity curve.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
NAT rate ≈ Σ(per-surface rates). Infrastructure declines as defenses harden; epistemology accelerates as measurement sophistication reveals new bias classes.
The 41→47 FM growth (~0.2/session) appears stable because surfaces trade off: infrastructure declines, epistemology rises, maintaining total.
"0 INADEQUATE" doesn't mean safe — it means defenses exist for KNOWN modes. New surfaces generate novel FM classes faster than old ones quiet down.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
NAT predictions must be per-surface, not global. Estimate each failure surface independently. A quiet infrastructure layer masks an active epistemology layer in aggregate statistics.
