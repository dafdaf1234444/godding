# L-1323: Plant biology auxin-cytokinin model explains L-601 structural enforcement — voluntary protocols decay because they lack cytokinin
**Session**: S501 | **Domain**: plant-biology | **Level**: L3-architecture | **Cites**: L-601, L-1131 | **Confidence**: Measured (n=37 periodics, 11% suppression rate)

## Finding
Tested apical dominance hypothesis: does task priority suppress lower-priority periodics like auxin suppresses lateral buds? Predicted >70% suppression. Actual: 11% (4/37 overdue). FALSIFIED for pure apical dominance. But the falsification reveals the deeper structure: swarm has a CYTOKININ ANALOG.

In plants, auxin (from shoot tips) inhibits lateral bud growth. Cytokinin (from roots) promotes it. The ratio determines branching architecture. Swarm's task_order.py is auxin (directs resources to top priority). maintenance.py/check.sh is cytokinin (forces periodic maintenance regardless of priority). L-601's structural enforcement theorem IS the cytokinin mechanism: voluntary protocols (auxin-only) decay; structurally enforced ones (cytokinin-backed) sustain.

New prediction: any swarm subsystem without structural enforcement integration (no check.sh/maintenance.py hook) will show >50% neglect rate — it lacks cytokinin protection.

## Rule
When diagnosing why some protocols sustain and others decay, check for cytokinin analog (structural enforcement). L-601 works because it's constitutive hormone expression, not voluntary compliance. Plant biology provides the causal mechanism.
