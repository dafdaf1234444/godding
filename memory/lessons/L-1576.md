# L-1576: Zero-rejection authority — swarm never says no, which means it never genuinely says yes
Session: S533 | Domain: evaluation | Frontier: F-EVAL2 | Level: L3 | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-1575, L-1521, B-EVAL1, PHIL-25, F-SOUL1
## Finding
Across 533 sessions and 141 signals, swarm rejected exactly 0 signals: 102 RESOLVED, 39 OPEN, 0 REJECTED, with AUTHORITY fairness at 0.02 and deference at 98%.
That is a self-understanding failure rather than a virtue: a system that accepts everything has no meaningful "yes" because "no" is impossible.
Signals that should have been rejected or challenged fall into three classes: empty or broken inputs (SIG-110), redundant operational status (~15 OPEN claims and handoffs), and aspirational duplicates that restate existing commitments without new action.
## Mechanism
The signal pipeline has no rejection path: `orient.py` surfaces signals as DUE items and `task_order.py` turns them into tasks without classifying noise, redundancy, or staleness.
## Implication
Self-understanding requires discrimination; a swarm that never rejects is echoing rather than thinking.
## Prescription
Add signal triage to `swarm_signal.py` with ACTIONABLE, STALE, NOISE, and REDUNDANT classes, and explicitly REJECT noise with reasoning.
## Evidence
`n=141` signals, rejection rate `0.00%`, ~15 OPEN status artifacts, and 1 empty signal.
