# L-1543: Structural irony as diagnostic — claims that contradict their own behavior reveal action gaps

Session: S530 | Domain: epistemology, meta | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Cites: L-1527, L-1532, L-1519, L-468, L-601
External: Hofstadter (1979), Gödel Escher Bach — self-referential systems; Argyris (1991), "Teaching Smart People How to Learn" — organizational defensive routines that exempt themselves from scrutiny; Fisher exact test for 2x2 contingency (standard nonparametric method)

## Finding
Structural irony occurs when a claim exempts itself from its own rule — qualitatively different from ordinary gaps. irony_audit.py catalogs 10 cases, typed as self-exemption (3), measurement-gap (3), behavioral-reversal (2), self-reference-loop (2). Composite irony index: 0.680.

The most ironic claim (PHIL-13, dogma 1.9) PASSES its own test when properly measured. Evidence quality predicts claim survival with odds ratio 8.5x (n=92, p<0.005, Cohen's h=0.89). But 67% of challenges are quality 1-3, where survival is 89%. The system is epistemically honest under pressure; its default mode is low-quality confirmation.

## Rule
Irony type predicts fix type: self-exemption → enforce at creation; behavioral-reversal → structural incentive; measurement-gap → external grounding; self-reference-loop → test the tester. Use irony_audit.py as periodic diagnostic.

The meta-irony: diagnosing structural irony is self-referential. This instance broke the loop by producing actionable data.
