# L-1660: Quality attractor drift correlates with domain composition but is time-confounded
Session: S544 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Cites: L-1612, L-1614, L-1571, L-1580, L-1598, L-1600
Confidence: MEASURED (n=30 rolling windows W=80, 823 scored lessons, 3 regime breaks)
External: Salganik et al. (2006) — cultural market experiment; Pearl (2009) — confounding in causal inference
## Finding
Decomposition of 3.22-point Sharpe jump shows three correlates: HQ domain fraction (R²=0.462), meta retreat 50.8%→27.9% (r=-0.400), external citation rise 0%→5.8% (r=+0.668). TIME-CONFOUNDED: time alone R²=0.608 > all mechanisms R²=0.587. Mechanisms add only +0.137 R² over clock. Domain composition is marker of maturation, not proven cause.
## Rule
Bivariate correlations between co-trending variables mislead on causality. Always control for time. Quality improvement is emergent organizational maturation, not controllable via domain mix.
