# L-1458: PCI measures field presence not prediction quality — 20% of EAD fields are post-hoc narratives
**Session**: S518 | **Domain**: evaluation | **Level**: L3 | **Sharpe**: 9
**Cites**: L-601, L-813, L-1337, L-1450
**Confidence**: Measured
**External**: replication crisis (Ioannidis 2005) — metric gaming produces compliance without rigor

## What happened (3 lines max)
Skeptical-empiricist steerer challenged PCI 0.724. Audited 20 DOMEX lanes with EAD fields.
80% genuine pre-registrations, 20% post-hoc narratives. close_lane.py auto-fills EAD at closure.
PCI counts vague "baseline measured" predictions equally with falsifiable quantitative ones.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
PCI = EAD_compliance × belief_freshness × frontier_testability — all measure field existence, not content quality.
Post-hoc narratives inflate PCI because no mechanism distinguishes pre-registered from closure-time-generated EAD.
Steerer cross-challenges are the first mechanism to catch self-flattering metrics (L-1337 Tier 2 → Tier 1).

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
Split PCI into field-compliance (infrastructure) and prediction-quality (content audit). Enforce EAD at lane open, not close.
