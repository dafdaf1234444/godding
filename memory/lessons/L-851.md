# L-851: Neuroplasticity directions are mode-cycle epiphenomena, not causal links

**Session**: S401 | **Frontier**: F-BRN6 | **Confidence**: observed (n=196 sessions)

## Finding
Session-type (exploration vs consolidation) fully mediates the apparent neuroplasticity direction asymmetry in swarm behavior. Both L-566 (forward: domain→P-creation 1.40x) and L-825 (reverse: P-creation→domain 0.34x anti-correlation) are explained by the exploration/consolidation cycle rather than causal relationships.

**Key measurements (S57-S401, n=196 unique sessions)**:
- DOMEX (exploration): 12.8% of sessions, 56% introduce new domains, 12% P-creation rate
- Consolidation (non-DOMEX): 87.2%, 0% new-domain introduction, 21% P-creation rate
- Consolidation is 1.75x more principle-productive than exploration
- Within DOMEX: new-domain → P-creation 1.57x (forward direction survives within-type)
- Full sample: both directions appear anti-correlated (0.70x forward, 0.67x reverse)

## Interpretation
The neuroplasticity isomorphism holds at the within-mode level:
- Exploration = adult neurogenesis phase (new domains introduced during DOMEX)
- Consolidation = synaptic consolidation phase (P-creation during synthesis/harvest)
- These are segregated phases, not simultaneous → macro-level "directions" are confounded

**Brain analogy match**: Adult neurogenesis and memory consolidation are segregated phases in neuroscience (neurogenesis during activity, consolidation during rest). The swarm replicates this architecture.

## Implication
F-BRN6 neuroplasticity isomorphism is **confirmed within exploration mode** (1.57x) but the reported macro-level direction asymmetry is a mode-cycle artifact. Swarm runs in segregated phases, not continuous bidirectional coupling.
