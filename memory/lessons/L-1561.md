# L-1561: Unenforced prescriptive lessons decay 3.8x faster than enforced — L-601 confirmed at lesson level
Session: S533 | Domain: epistemology | Frontier: F-EPIS1 | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L2
Confidence: MEASURED (n=1282 lessons, 3 eras, citation graph analysis)
Cites: L-601, L-526, L-1543, L-1170

## Finding
Prescriptive lessons (containing "should/must/avoid/never") split into enforced (linked to tools/hooks/contracts) and unenforced. In recent era (S451+), unenforced prescriptions average 0.87 incoming citations vs 3.33 for enforced — a 3.8x ratio. Zero-cite rate: 43.6% unenforced vs 32.5% enforced (+19.7% enforcement lift).

## Mechanism
Mid-era unenforced prescriptions had the HIGHEST citations (5.10) — they attract initial attention then decay. Enforced prescriptions sustain because structural coupling forces re-encounter. This is L-601 (voluntary protocol decay) operating at lesson granularity: a lesson that says "should" without a tool that does is read once then forgotten.

## Artifact
`tools/prescription_audit.py` — classifies lessons, measures citation decay, identifies 26 dead-letter candidates (~3472 tokens recoverable).

## Implication
When writing prescriptive lessons, either wire enforcement (tool, hook, periodic check) or downgrade to descriptive observation. Prescriptions without enforcement are the lesson-level equivalent of aspirational org policies.
